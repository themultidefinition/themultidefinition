TMD (The Multi-Definition)

TMD is the largest and most respected third-party authentication and grading company in Nedre Land for sound and music. Founded by Dr. Buzz Phylo, a famous doctor of acoustics, with the support of the Jabon Council.

TMD is committed to providing scientific and fair identification and rating services for sounds and music around the world, while promoting innovation and trade in sound art.

TMD's mission is to establish a standardized evaluation system for Nedre Land's music and sound industry through bioacoustics and audio signal processing technology. We not only focus on the artistry and cultural value of sound, but also analyze the essence and potential of sound from the perspectives of biology, physics and informatics. Whether it is a biological soundscape in nature or a musical work, TMD can provide multi-dimensional scientific identification and rating.

TMD's academic appraisal and technology application

Bioacoustic analysis

TMD uses advanced bioacoustic technology to analyze the interaction between sound and organisms. By accurately measuring the frequency, amplitude, harmonic structure, etc. of sound waves, we can evaluate the physiological and psychological effects of sound on organisms. TMD's research shows that sound waves can resonate with the cell structure of organisms, thereby affecting their physiological state. Using the combined analysis of Sonic Cell Modulator and Brainwave Synchronizer, TMD can more accurately systematically quantify the impact of sound on the cell activity of the evaluation source and the stimulation values ​​of different bands, providing more scientific data for the evaluation results.

Sound signal processing and artificial intelligence

TMD has developed an artificial intelligence-based sound signal processing system that can perform high-precision spectral analysis, emotion recognition and cultural interpretation of sound. Through deep learning algorithms, our system can identify emotional features in sound and evaluate its meaning and value in a specific cultural context.

Interdisciplinary research on sound waves and neuroscience

TMD cooperates with top institutions in the field of neuroscience to study the impact of sound waves on the human brain. Through electroencephalogram (EEG) and functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) technology, we can monitor the listener's neural response in real time when exposed to different sounds, thus providing scientific basis for music therapy, acoustic hypnosis and other fields.

Sound ecology and cultural heritage protection

TMD not only focuses on artificially created sounds, but also strives to protect natural soundscapes and sound cultural heritage. We use acoustic sensor networks to record and analyze natural soundscapes around the world (such as rainforests, oceans, deserts, etc.), and at the same time establish digital archives for endangered sound cultural heritage (such as traditional musical instruments, dialect songs, etc.).
